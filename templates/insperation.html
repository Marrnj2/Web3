Cool Websites and What I like About Them

Thrive

Thrive does a really good job at spliting is content into clear sections. It does this in a couple of ways. Firstly content on the page is seperated by large amounts of negative space. This helps the websites look by letting it be free of any clutter. It also helps users of the website navigate the pages. Since the content is seperated you know when content is begining and ending.

Status
Status uses its inital hero image with its navigation to set the color palete for the website. The color palete is really simple composed of only 3 main colors. The image backgrounds on the website also stick to the same pallete. The limited color pallet is the main aspect of Status I want to incorperate into my website. The limited use of color prevents the website from being overwheliming and interesting enough to stop it being bland.
Woop

The navigation bar followes the user around the webpage. This means you can always navigate to new areas of the website without having to scroll back to the top. Another thing about the navigation. It collapses down when the screen gets to small. This means those on devices with smaller screens can still easyly explore the webpage simply by expanding the nav menu. The thing about the collapsing nav is. when you expand it, it becomes a differeent version of navigation, leaving behind the more traditional style of navigation for a clean lookin list
